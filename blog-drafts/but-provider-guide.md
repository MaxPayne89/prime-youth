# How Activity Providers in Berlin Can Accept Government Subsidies — and Why It Will Grow Your Bookings

If you run an afterschool programme, sports club, music school, or holiday camp in Berlin, there is a large and largely untapped group of families who want to book with you — but cannot afford to without government support.

The **Bildung und Teilhabe (BuT)** programme gives eligible children up to **€180 per year** for exactly the kind of activities you provide. The reason those families are not booking with you yet is almost never about interest. It is almost always about not knowing how the process works — or not finding providers who openly accept BuT.

This guide explains what BuT means for providers, how to accept it, and how a school partnership can unlock even larger subsidies for your families.

---

## What Is BuT and Why Should Providers Care?

Bildung und Teilhabe is Germany's federal education and participation package for children from lower-income families. Administered locally by Jobcenters, Sozialämter, and — for asylum seekers in Berlin — the LAF (Landesamt für Flüchtlingsangelegenheiten), it covers activities including:

- Sports clubs and weekly training sessions
- Music, art, and theatre classes
- Afterschool programmes and homework clubs
- Holiday camps and multi-day residential trips
- School excursions and class trips

The money does not go to families — it goes **directly to you, the provider**. The relevant authority (Jobcenter, Sozialamt, or LAF) pays your invoice once a family submits an approved claim.

For providers, this means:

- **Guaranteed payment** — you are paid by a government authority, not chasing individual families
- **Wider reach** — you become accessible to families who previously could not afford your programmes
- **Lower dropout** — financially supported families are less likely to cancel mid-term due to cost

## 7 Steps to Start Accepting BuT Payments

### 1. Understand Which BuT Category Applies to Your Programme

There are two main BuT categories relevant to activity providers:

**Category 1 — Social and Cultural Participation (§28 Abs. 7 SGB II)**
Covers ongoing activities like weekly clubs, classes, and memberships. Families receive €15 per month (up to €180/year) paid directly to you. This applies to the vast majority of afterschool and extracurricular providers.

**Category 2 — Multi-Day Educational Trips (§28 Abs. 2 SGB II)**
Covers school trips, class excursions, and school-partnered multi-day programmes at **full actual cost** — no cap. This applies if you have a formal cooperation agreement with a school (more on this below).

Most providers start with Category 1 and layer in Category 2 as they build school relationships.

---

### 2. Make Sure Your Programme Qualifies

For Category 1, your activity must be:
- **Guided and structured** — not informal free time or drop-in sessions
- **Led by a qualified or responsible adult**
- **For children under 18** (up to 25 for those still in education)
- Clearly described: what it is, how often it meets, and what it costs

Sports clubs, music lessons, art classes, coding camps, dance programmes, robotics workshops, language courses — all qualify. If you are unsure, contact your local Jobcenter for a pre-check.

---

### 3. Prepare a BuT-Compatible Invoice

This is the most common sticking point. Authorities are particular about what invoices must contain. Your invoice to the Jobcenter or Sozialamt must include:

- Your full legal name or organisation name
- Your address
- Your tax ID (Steuernummer) or VAT number (USt-IdNr.), if applicable
- The child's full name and date of birth
- A clear description of the activity (what it is, where, when, how often)
- The cost per month or per programme
- The total amount being claimed
- Your bank details (IBAN and BIC)
- The period being covered

Authorities will reject invoices missing any of these fields. If you use a booking platform, check whether it generates compliant invoices automatically — this saves significant administrative time.

---

### 4. Train Your Team to Handle BuT Enquiries

When a family contacts you about BuT, your front desk or admin team needs to know:

- Which authorities cover which benefit types (Jobcenter for Bürgergeld, Sozialamt for Sozialhilfe, LAF for asylum seekers, Wohnungsamt for Wohngeld)
- That the family needs to apply to their authority **before** the activity starts — not after
- That you will provide a booking confirmation and invoice, and the authority pays you directly
- That the family does not pay you — do not invoice the family for BuT-covered amounts

A one-page internal reference sheet covering these points is usually enough.

---

### 5. Set Up a Separate BuT Billing Track

BuT payments come from government authorities on different timelines than regular card or bank transfer payments. You need to track:

- Which enrolled children are BuT-funded
- Which authority is responsible for each child
- Which invoices have been submitted and to which office
- Which payments are outstanding and from whom

Keep this separate from your standard payment tracking. Some activity management platforms handle this automatically; if you manage bookings manually, a simple spreadsheet with columns for authority name, contact, invoice date, and payment status is the minimum you need.

---

### 6. Submit Your Invoices Correctly

Once a family has their BuT approval (Bewilligungsbescheid) and has enrolled, you invoice the relevant authority — not the family. Key points:

- Send invoices to the correct office (each district has its own Jobcenter branch and Sozialamt)
- Include the family's case reference number (Aktenzeichen) on the invoice — this is critical for processing
- Submit monthly for ongoing activities, or once per programme for camps and one-off events
- Keep copies of all submitted invoices and payment confirmations

Payment typically arrives within **4 to 6 weeks** of invoice submission for established claims. For new families, allow additional time for the initial approval process.

---

### 7. Display That You Accept BuT

This sounds simple but it is frequently overlooked. Families searching for BuT-eligible activities are specifically looking for providers who accept it. Make it visible:

- Add "BuT-accepted" or "Bildung und Teilhabe willkommen" to your website, social media profiles, and any printed materials
- Include it in your booking confirmation emails: "This programme is eligible for Bildung und Teilhabe funding. Contact us for details."
- List your programme on platforms that filter by BuT acceptance

Families who have BuT entitlement are often specifically searching for providers who display this — your competitors who do not mention it are invisible to this audience.

## Going Further: The School Partnership Advantage

If you want to unlock the second, uncapped BuT category — full cost coverage for multi-day educational trips — you need a **formal school cooperation agreement** (*Kooperationsvertrag mit einer Schule*).

Here is what that unlocks:

| Without school partnership | With school partnership |
|---|---|
| €15/month per child (Category 1) | Full actual cost per trip (Category 2) |
| Families apply individually | School may coordinate applications collectively |
| Limited to ongoing activities | Covers residential camps and multi-day trips |
| €180/year maximum per child | No upper cost limit per trip |

Germany's Federal Social Court has confirmed that school-partnered programmes qualify for Category 2 coverage even when delivered by external private providers — the deciding factor is the formal educational relationship, not whether the activity takes place on school grounds.

**How to set up a school partnership:**

1. Identify two or three local Berlin schools whose pupils already attend or would benefit from your programmes
2. Contact the school principal (Schulleiter) or administrator to propose a Kooperationsvertrag
3. The agreement is typically a simple one or two page document stating that your provider is a recognised educational partner of the school
4. Register the agreement with the relevant Schulamt if required
5. Update your BuT documentation to reference the partnership — this changes which legal basis applies to your invoices

Many schools are actively looking for quality external programme providers, particularly for the expanding *Ganztagsschule* (all-day school) model. Your afterschool programme could become a formally funded part of a school's extended provision — paid per semester directly to you.

## The Business Case in Numbers

Consider a provider running a weekly robotics club at €40/month per child:

- Without BuT promotion: accessible mainly to families who can self-fund
- With BuT Category 1: families pay nothing; Jobcenter pays €40/month directly to provider (within the €15/month BuT limit, families may contribute a small top-up — the BuT covers up to €15, the family covers the rest, or you adjust pricing to fit within the threshold)
- With school partnership + Category 2: a residential robotics camp at €200 is fully covered for eligible children — no parental contribution at all

The administrative overhead of handling BuT invoices is real but manageable. The return is a wider, more stable, and more diverse customer base — and the satisfaction of making your programme genuinely accessible.
