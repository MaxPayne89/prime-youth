# What Is Bildung und Teilhabe (BuT)? A Complete Guide for Families in Berlin

If your child has ever had to miss out on a sports club, music lessons, or a school trip because of the cost, there is a government benefit in Germany designed exactly for this situation — and thousands of eligible Berlin families are not claiming it.

It is called **Bildung und Teilhabe**, or BuT for short. Translated literally as "Education and Participation," it is a federal package of benefits that covers everything from school supplies and class trips to afterschool activities and summer camps. Here is everything you need to know.

---

## What Is Bildung und Teilhabe?

Bildung und Teilhabe is a set of benefits under German social law (SGB II, SGB XII, and AsylbLG) that ensures children from lower-income families can fully participate in school and social life. It is not a charity — it is a legal entitlement. If your family qualifies, you have a right to claim it.

The BuT package covers six main areas:

- **School trips and class excursions** — day trips and multi-day travel
- **School supplies** — twice yearly contributions toward school materials
- **Transport to school** — where the school is not within walking distance
- **School meals** — subsidised hot meals at school or daycare
- **Learning support (Lernförderung)** — tutoring and extra academic help
- **Social and cultural participation** — sports clubs, music lessons, afterschool activities, summer camps

This guide focuses on that last category — the one most relevant to afterschool activities and camps for children in Berlin.

---

## Who Qualifies for BuT?

Your child is likely eligible if your family receives any of the following:

1. **Bürgergeld** (formerly Hartz IV / SGB II) — administered by your local Jobcenter
2. **Wohngeld** (housing benefit) — administered by the Wohnungsamt
3. **Kinderzuschlag** (child supplement) — administered by the Familienkasse
4. **Sozialhilfe** (SGB XII) — administered by your local Sozialamt
5. **Asylbewerberleistungen** (asylum seeker benefits) — administered by the LAF (Landesamt für Flüchtlingsangelegenheiten) in Berlin

Children up to the age of **18** are eligible. For some benefits, the upper age limit extends to 25 for young people still in education.

---

## How Much Is the Social and Cultural Participation Benefit?

For afterschool activities, sports clubs, music lessons, and similar programs, BuT provides a flat rate of **€15 per month**, or **up to €180 per year** per child.

### 5 Things Most Families Don't Know About the €15 Monthly Benefit

1. **You do not have to use it every month.** Unused months accumulate. If your child does nothing from January to June, you have €90 available by July — enough to contribute significantly toward a summer camp.

2. **It pays the provider directly.** The money does not go into your bank account. Your local authority pays the activity provider directly on your behalf. You simply present your BuT approval and the activity takes place.

3. **It covers a wide range of activities.** Football clubs, dance classes, music lessons, art courses, theatre groups, swimming classes, and organised afterschool programs all qualify. The activity must be guided and structured — not just informal free time.

4. **You do not need to reapply every month.** Once approved, ongoing activities are usually covered automatically until the benefit period ends or the activity changes.

5. **It can be combined with other funding.** The €15/month benefit for social participation can run at the same time as other BuT benefits (such as school trip coverage), as long as they are for different activities.

## How to Apply for BuT in Berlin

The application process depends on which benefit your family receives:

### If you receive Bürgergeld
Contact your **Jobcenter** directly. You do not submit a separate application — you submit proof of the activity and cost, and the Jobcenter approves payment to the provider.

### If you receive Wohngeld or Kinderzuschlag
Apply online at **service.berlin.de** (reference: Dienstleistung 324466). You will need to upload supporting documents in PDF, JPG, or PNG format. Total file size must be under 30MB.

### If you receive Sozialhilfe
Contact your local **Sozialamt** (district social welfare office). Each Berlin district (Bezirk) has its own office.

### If you receive Asylbewerberleistungen
Contact the **LAF** (Landesamt für Flüchtlingsangelegenheiten) at Darwinstraße 14–18 (entrance: Goslarer Ufer 15). The LAF administers BuT for asylum seekers and refugees in Berlin under §3 Abs. 4 AsylbLG — the same social and cultural participation benefit applies.

---

## What Documents Will You Need?

Regardless of which office handles your case, you will typically need:

- Proof of your current benefit entitlement (your Bescheid)
- Your child's name and date of birth
- Details of the activity: provider name, address, description, cost, and dates
- A confirmation from the activity provider (an invoice or booking confirmation)

The activity provider — such as a sports club or afterschool programme — will usually have experience with BuT and can help you gather the right documentation.

---

## Common Reasons Families Miss Out

Despite being a legal entitlement, BuT is significantly under-claimed. The most common reasons:

- **Not knowing it exists.** Many families, particularly those who are new to Germany, are simply not aware of the benefit.
- **Assuming the paperwork is too complicated.** In many cases, the provider handles most of the documentation.
- **Thinking it only covers school costs.** The social participation element (afterschool activities, clubs, camps) is the least well-known part of BuT.
- **Missing the application window.** BuT can sometimes be claimed retroactively, but it is much easier to apply before the activity starts.

---

## Key Takeaway

If your family receives any means-tested benefit in Germany, your child almost certainly qualifies for up to **€180 per year** toward afterschool activities, sports clubs, music lessons, or a summer camp. The application can be done online in Berlin, and in many cases the provider handles the paperwork on your behalf.

Do not let cost be the reason your child misses out.
