# Can the German Government Pay for Your Child's Summer Camp? Yes — Here's How

Summer camps in Germany can cost anywhere from €150 for a week-long local programme to over €1,000 for a residential multi-week experience. For families on lower incomes, that price tag puts summer camps firmly out of reach.

What most families do not know is that the German government has a benefit specifically designed to change that.

Here are seven things you need to know about using **Bildung und Teilhabe (BuT)** to fund your child's summer camp in Berlin.

---

## 1. The BuT Programme Specifically Covers Holiday Activities

Bildung und Teilhabe is Germany's federal education and participation package for children from lower-income families. One of its six benefit categories — **Teilhabe am sozialen und kulturellen Leben** (social and cultural participation) — directly covers:

- Summer and holiday camps
- Sports clubs and weekly activity classes
- Music, art, and theatre programmes
- Organised day trips and excursions
- Youth group activities

If your family receives Bürgergeld, Wohngeld, Kinderzuschlag, Sozialhilfe, or Asylbewerberleistungen, your child almost certainly qualifies.

---

## 2. You Get Up to €180 Per Year — and You Can Save It Up

The standard BuT social participation benefit is **€15 per month**, totalling **€180 per year** per child. But here is the part most families miss:

**You do not have to spend it monthly.** Unused months accumulate.

This means a family that has not claimed the benefit from January through June has **€90 available** by July — enough to cover a significant portion of a summer camp, or even the full cost of a shorter local programme.

**Example:**
> The Yilmaz family receives Bürgergeld. Their daughter has not attended any paid clubs during the school year. In August they find a one-week robotics camp costing €85. They apply their accumulated BuT allowance (six months × €15 = €90). The Jobcenter pays the camp provider €85 directly. Their daughter attends for free.

This is exactly how BuT is designed to work — and it is entirely legitimate.

---

## 3. There Are Two Different BuT Benefits for Camps — and One Has No Cap

Most people only know about the €15/month benefit. But there is a second, separate BuT category that covers **multi-day educational trips** — and it has **no upper cost limit**.

Under **§28 Abs. 2 SGB II**, if a camp or trip is organised by a school or in formal partnership with a school, the **full actual cost** is covered. Not €15. Not €180. The entire bill.

This applies when:
- The programme is organised by a school directly (a class trip, school excursion, or school project week)
- The provider has a formal cooperation agreement (*Kooperationsvertrag*) with a school
- The activity fulfils a recognised educational or pedagogical mandate

Germany's Federal Social Court (Bundessozialgericht) has confirmed that the definition of "school trip" is interpreted broadly — a circus project week held on school grounds, for example, qualifies as a school trip for BuT purposes.

**The practical implication:** If your child's school organises a trip to an external camp or activity provider, ask your Jobcenter about full cost coverage — not just the €15/month benefit.

---

## 4. The Money Goes to the Provider, Not to You

BuT is not a cash payment to families. The local authority (Jobcenter, Sozialamt, or LAF) pays the activity provider directly. As a family, you:

1. Apply for BuT and receive an approval letter (Bewilligungsbescheid)
2. Show this to the camp or activity provider when booking
3. The provider invoices the authority
4. The authority pays the provider
5. Your child attends

You do not handle the money at any point. This also means you cannot "save up" the cash — only the entitlement accumulates, and it is applied directly to a specific activity cost.

---

## 5. The Application Process in Berlin Is Now Partially Online

In Berlin, where you apply depends on which benefit your family receives:

| Your benefit | Who handles it | How to apply |
|---|---|---|
| Bürgergeld (SGB II) | Your Jobcenter branch | Submit proof of activity in person or by post |
| Wohngeld / Kinderzuschlag | Wohnungsamt / Familienkasse | Online at service.berlin.de (Ref: 324466) |
| Sozialhilfe (SGB XII) | Your district Sozialamt | In person or by post |
| Asylbewerberleistungen | LAF Berlin | In person at Goslarer Ufer 15 |

For the online application (Wohngeld/Kinderzuschlag recipients), you upload documents as PDF, JPG, or PNG files — total size under 30MB. This can be done from home without visiting an office.

**Apply before the camp starts.** While some retroactive claims are possible, the process is significantly smoother when you apply in advance.

---

## 6. What Documentation You Will Need

The camp or activity provider will usually help with this, but you should be prepared with:

- Your current benefit approval letter (Bewilligungsbescheid) showing your entitlement
- Your child's full name and date of birth
- A booking confirmation or invoice from the camp provider, including: provider name and address, activity description, dates, cost, and bank details
- For school-organised trips: a letter or confirmation from the school

Good activity platforms and camp providers generate this documentation automatically at the point of booking, so you do not have to chase paperwork.

---

## 7. Many Families Qualify But Never Claim

Research consistently shows that BuT is significantly under-claimed across Germany. In Berlin alone, an estimated large proportion of eligible children never access the social participation benefit — not because they are ineligible, but because:

- They did not know the benefit existed
- They assumed the process was too complicated
- They thought it only covered school-related costs
- No one at the activity provider told them it was available

If you are not sure whether your family qualifies, contact your local Jobcenter, Sozialamt, or the LAF. Eligibility checks are free and non-binding.

## Quick Reference: BuT Summer Camp Checklist

- [ ] Check which benefit your family receives (Bürgergeld, Wohngeld, Kinderzuschlag, etc.)
- [ ] Find out how much accumulated BuT entitlement your child has (ask your relevant office)
- [ ] Choose a camp or activity that accepts BuT payment
- [ ] Ask the provider for a BuT-compatible invoice or booking confirmation
- [ ] Submit your application to the correct Berlin authority before the camp starts
- [ ] Keep a copy of your approval letter to show the provider
